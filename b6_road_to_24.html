The Improvement Loop That Never Stops Working

From 54 to 24, improvement is not mysterious: measure → find the biggest leak → practise that for a month → re-measure. This guide sets up the loop; the whole playbook library exists to feed it.

The Loop

Four Steps, Repeated Monthly

  • 1MEASURE: after each round record five numbers — putts, up-and-downs attempted/made, fairways-ish, penalties+duffs, blow-up holes.
  • 2DIAGNOSE: which number is furthest from the milestone table (next tab)? That is the leak. (The free SG Diagnostic automates this.)
  • 3TRAIN: one month, two-thirds of practice time on the leak, one-third maintaining everything else.
  • 4RE-MEASURE and repeat. The leak moves — putting one quarter, contact the next. That is normal and good.
The Time Budget

What Progress Costs at This Level

From the mid-40s to 24, the going rate is roughly: 10 minutes of daily short-game/putting at home, one range session and one round (or two 9s) a week. Miss a week? The loop does not care; it restarts where it left off. The 30-Day Score Blueprint (free download on the site) is one full lap of this loop, day by day.

Your First Season Plan

A realistic 6-month arc from first sets to a falling handicap.

Months 1–2

Foundations

Guides b1–b3: kit sorted, three lessons, towel drill, home short-game circuit, first par-3 loops. Success = clean contact more often than not.

Months 3–4

Real Golf Begins

Guides b4–b5: first full courses, ready-golf habits, iGolf or club membership, first three cards submitted, index live. The five-number tracking starts. Success = an official handicap and no pace anxiety.

Months 5–6

The Loop Runs

Guide b6 + the 30-Day Score Blueprint as month five. Re-measure, pick the next leak, repeat for month six. Success = the index moving down and you knowing exactly why. From here, the main library — and if you eventually want the full practice architecture, launch-monitor protocols and DECADE-style strategy systems, that is what Pro is for.
