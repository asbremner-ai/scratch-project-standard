Privacy Policy

Last updated: June 2026 · Version 1.0

1. Who We Are

The Scratch Project ("we", "us", "our") is operated as a sole trader business based in the United Kingdom. We publish golf improvement content at asbremner-ai.github.io/scratch-project-standard and sell digital products via Gumroad. This Privacy Policy explains how we collect, use, and protect your personal information.

For privacy enquiries, contact us at: hello@thescratchproject.co.uk

2. Information We Collect

We collect different types of information depending on how you interact with us:

  • Purchase information: When you buy a product through Gumroad, Gumroad collects your name, email address, and payment information. We receive your name, email, and purchase details from Gumroad to fulfil your order and provide support.
  • Email address: If you subscribe to our email list via ConvertKit, we collect and store your email address and any information you provide at sign-up.
  • Usage data: Our website is hosted on GitHub Pages, which may collect standard server logs including IP addresses and browser information. We do not control or access this data directly.
  • Communications: If you contact us by email, we retain the content of that communication to respond to your query.

We do not use cookies on this website. We do not use tracking pixels, behavioural advertising, or analytics services beyond GitHub Pages' standard server logs.

3. How We Use Your Information

We use the information we collect to:

  • Fulfil your product purchase and deliver your download
  • Send you email communications you have opted into (product updates, tips, launch announcements)
  • Respond to support queries and questions
  • Comply with legal obligations

We do not sell, rent, or share your personal information with third parties for marketing purposes.

4. Our Third-Party Services

We use the following third-party services to operate our business. Each has their own privacy policy and acts as a data processor on our behalf:

  • Gumroad (gumroad.com) — payment processing and product delivery. We have activated the Gumroad Data Processing Agreement. Gumroad's privacy policy: gumroad.com/privacy
  • ConvertKit (convertkit.com) — email marketing and list management. We have activated the ConvertKit Data Processing Agreement. ConvertKit's privacy policy: convertkit.com/privacy
  • GitHub Pages (github.com) — website hosting. GitHub's privacy statement: GitHub Privacy Statement

5. Legal Basis for Processing (UK/EU)

Under UK GDPR and the Data Protection Act 2018, we process your personal data on the following legal bases:

  • Contract performance: Processing your purchase and delivering your product
  • Legitimate interests: Responding to your support queries
  • Consent: Sending marketing emails — you may unsubscribe at any time using the link in any email we send

6. Data Retention

We retain your data for as long as necessary to fulfil the purposes outlined in this policy:

  • Purchase records: Retained for 7 years for tax and legal compliance
  • Email list: Until you unsubscribe or request deletion
  • Support communications: 2 years from the date of last contact

7. Your Rights

Under UK GDPR, you have the right to:

  • Access the personal data we hold about you
  • Rectification — correct inaccurate personal data
  • Erasure — request deletion of your personal data (subject to legal retention requirements)
  • Restriction — ask us to pause processing of your data in certain circumstances
  • Data portability — receive your data in a machine-readable format
  • Object to processing based on legitimate interests
  • Withdraw consent at any time for consent-based processing

To exercise any of these rights, contact us at hello@thescratchproject.co.uk. We will respond within 30 days. You also have the right to lodge a complaint with the Information Commissioner's Office (ICO) at ico.org.uk.

8. International Transfers

Gumroad and ConvertKit are US-based companies. Both have appropriate data transfer mechanisms in place (Standard Contractual Clauses) for transfers of personal data outside the UK/EEA. Details are available in their respective privacy policies.

9. Children's Privacy

Our products and services are not directed at children under 16. We do not knowingly collect personal data from children. If you believe a child has provided us with personal data, please contact us and we will delete it.

10. Changes to This Policy

We may update this Privacy Policy from time to time. We will notify email subscribers of material changes. The "last updated" date at the top of this page will always reflect the most recent version. Continued use of our services after changes constitutes acceptance of the updated policy.
