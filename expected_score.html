How the Model Works

Every assumption stated openly — this tool's credibility is its honesty. It is a simple statistical model, not a swing analyser.

Step 1 — Course Handicap

The WHS Formula

Course handicap = round( Index × Slope ÷ 113 + (Course Rating − Par) ). Identical to the Playing Handicap Calculator — the two never disagree.

Step 2 — Expected Average (the K correction)

Your Handicap Is Your Potential, Not Your Average

Your WHS handicap is built from your best 8 of your last 20 rounds — so your average round is not your handicap, it's several strokes worse. This tool uses expected average = par + course handicap + 3.

⚑ FLAGGED FOR REVIEW — the "+3" (K): the repo has no direct average-vs-handicap figure, so this uses the well-documented WHS/USGA/Shot Scope value. Because handicaps track the best 8 of 20, the average round runs ~2–4 strokes over course handicap; +3 produces a ~1-in-5 beat-handicap rate, which matches published data. Adjust here if a better in-repo source appears.
Step 3 — The Distribution

Good Day, Typical, Rough Day

Round scores are modelled as a bell curve around the expected average, with a spread (σ) of about 2.7 strokes for low handicaps rising to ~4 for high — higher handicaps are more variable. A good day (best ~20%) is roughly the average minus 0.85σ; a rough day (worst ~20%) is the average plus 0.85σ.

⚑ σ is also a sourced estimate (Shot Scope / WHS score-differential spread), not a fitted per-player value. It's flagged like K above.
Step 4 — Beat-Handicap Odds & Blow-Ups

The Two Most Expectation-Correcting Numbers

Beating your handicap happens roughly 1 round in 4–5 — that's the WHS system working exactly as designed, not you failing. And every handicap band has a typical number of double-bogey-or-worse holes per round (from the Rounds Tracker benchmarks): expecting zero is the fastest route to a ruined round. A score 7.0 strokes better than your handicap triggers an exceptional-score reduction — rare by design.
