Learn the Game Backwards

Half of all your shots happen within 100 yards of the hole — and for a new golfer closer to 60%. Tour players learned green-to-tee as juniors: putt first, then chip, then pitch, then full swing. It is the fastest route to enjoying golf, because short-game competence turns 9-shot holes into 6-shot holes without a single better drive.

Why This Works

The Maths of the Beginner Round

A new golfer on a par-4: three shots to get near the green, then — with no short game — a duffed chip, a skulled chip, three putts: 8. Same three shots plus one decent chip and two putts: 6. Nothing about the "real golf swing" changed; the score dropped by two per hole. Short game is also learnable at home, in winter, in ten-minute blocks, with instant feedback.

Putting — Two Skills Only

Speed first, start-line second. Everything else in putting is decoration until you break 90.

Speed: The Ladder

Feel Before Technique

  • 1On the practice green, put tees at 10, 20 and 30 feet.
  • 2Three balls to each: success = past the hole but inside 3 feet.
  • 3Score out of 9, write it down, beat it next visit.
  • 4Rule of thumb on the course: aim to die every putt just past the front edge — never "give it a chance" from 30 feet.
Start-Line: The Gate

A Carpet Drill That Transfers

Two tees (or two coins at home) a ball-and-a-half apart, five feet from a target. Fifteen putts through the gate per session. The stroke that starts the ball on line is short, smooth and finishes holding the follow-through — like sliding a book along a shelf, not hitting.

Green Etiquette Micro-Lesson

While You Practise

Never walk on the line between another ball and the hole; mark your ball with a coin if asked; the player furthest away putts first. Three habits, learned on the practice green, that make you welcome in any group — more in Guide b4.

Chipping — One Club, One Shape

Forget flop shots. The beginner short game is one reliable, boring, ground-hugging shot: the bump-and-run.

The Bump-and-Run

Your Get-Out-of-Jail Shot

  • 1Take a 9-iron (or 8). Narrow stance, ball centre, weight 60% on the lead side, hands slightly ahead.
  • 2Make a putting-stroke-sized swing — shoulders rock, wrists quiet.
  • 3Land the ball a third of the way to the hole and let it run the rest.
  • 4Practise to a towel (the landing spot), not the hole — landing spot is the skill.
The Disaster Filter

Before Any Short Shot, Ask One Question

"What is the worst that happens if I miss this slightly?" If the answer involves a bunker, water or the ball racing past the flag down a slope — aim somewhere safer. The up-and-down can wait; the triple bogey cannot. This one habit is worth more shots than any technique on this page.

Ten Minutes a Day

The Home Circuit

Five minutes gate-drill putts on the carpet, five minutes bump-and-run setup rehearsals into a cushion or net (or slow-motion swings brushing the same carpet spot).
