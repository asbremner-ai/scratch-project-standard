Tracking Technology
Choice Guide

Arccos vs Golfmetrics vs GPS apps vs manual tracking — which tool is right for your situation, commitment level, and budget? This guide cuts through the marketing and gives you a framework for choosing based on how you actually play and practise.

📡 Arccos Air🗂️ Golfmetrics📱 GPS Apps📋 Manual💡 Decision Framework

The Right Tool for You

The best tracking tool is the one you will actually use consistently for 6+ months. The most statistically sophisticated system you abandon after 8 rounds is worth nothing. This decision framework starts with your situation, not the spec sheet.

Decision Framework — Start Here
Four Questions That Determine Your Tool

Answer These Before Reading Anything Else

  • 1
    How many competitive rounds do you play per month? Under 2: manual tracking is sufficient. 2–4: GPS app with partial SG. 4+: full SG tracking (Arccos or Golfmetrics) produces the most value because you accumulate enough data to identify patterns.
  • 2
    How much mental bandwidth can you commit during a round? If you find tracking distracting, automatic solutions (Arccos) are better than manual ones. If you enjoy the process of recording data, manual or Golfmetrics works well.
  • 3
    Do you have a coach? If yes, full SG data (Arccos or Golfmetrics) gives your coach something actionable to work from. GPS app data is much harder for a coach to use for diagnosis.
  • 4
    What is your budget? Arccos Air is a subscription (~£10–12/month). Golfmetrics is free with optional paid tier. GPS apps range from free to ~£30/year. Manual tracking is free.

Arccos Air — Full Guide

Arccos Air uses Bluetooth sensors in your grips plus a phone app to automatically track every shot — club, location, and outcome — without any manual input during the round.

How It Works
The Automatic Tracking System

Setup → Track → Analyse

  • 1
    Setup (one-time, 30 min): Screw small Bluetooth sensors into the grip end of every club (they are invisible from above). Connect to the Arccos Caddie app. Walk the first fairway and the system begins tracking automatically.
  • 2
    During round: Your phone stays in your pocket. The app detects each swing via Bluetooth and GPS. After the round, review shot data, agree/edit where needed, and the app calculates your SG in all categories.
  • 3
    Analytics: Rolling SG averages by category, strokes gained per club, proximity averages, trend graphs. After 8+ rounds, patterns become statistically meaningful. The system also provides in-round AI caddie recommendations (club, aim point, risk/reward).
Limitations to Know
Where Arccos Falls Short

What the Marketing Doesn't Tell You

  • !
    Accuracy requires correction: The app occasionally misses shots or misidentifies clubs. Post-round review and correction is essential for accurate SG data — expect 5–10 minutes per round. Uncorrected data is unreliable.
  • !
    No lie quality data: Arccos records where the ball ended up, not how difficult the lie was. Golfmetrics captures lie quality (fairway, rough, hardpan) which significantly improves the accuracy of SG: ARG analysis.
  • !
    Subscription dependency: Your data is accessible only via the Arccos platform. If you cancel, access to historical data becomes limited. Export your data annually as a backup.

Golfmetrics — Full Guide

Golfmetrics is the most analytically rigorous manual tracking system available for amateur golfers. It captures lie quality alongside shot outcomes — producing more accurate SG: ARG data than any automated system.

What Makes It Different
Lie Quality — The Key Differentiator

Why Golfmetrics Produces More Accurate Short Game Data

Every SG: ARG calculation depends on knowing where you started the shot from — not just the GPS location, but the lie type. A chip from tight fairway grass and a chip from heavy rough 5 yards from the green are fundamentally different shots with different expected outcomes. Arccos cannot capture this distinction. Golfmetrics does.

The practical result: Golfmetrics ARG data is significantly more actionable than Arccos ARG data for diagnosing short game weaknesses, because it separates shots by lie difficulty.

Getting Started With Golfmetrics
Setup Guide

First Round Protocol

  • 1
    Download the Golfmetrics app (iOS and Android, free). Create an account. Select your home course from the database.
  • 2
    During the round: After each shot, tap the app to record: club used, outcome (fairway/rough/GIR/missed left etc.), and for shots within 100 yards, the lie type. The process takes 5–10 seconds per shot once familiar.
  • 3
    After 8 rounds: Review your SG category averages. The categories with the most negative SG are your practice priorities. Feed these into Guide 10 (Your Milestone Plan) to align practice with your actual data.

GPS Apps — When They Are Enough

GPS apps (Hole19, 18Birdies, Golf GPS) provide yardages, hazard distances, and basic stats. They do not provide full SG data. They are the right tool for some players and the wrong tool for others.

These stats are directionally useful but not precise. They can tell you where to focus but not by how much. Full SG data (Arccos or Golfmetrics) is required for precise diagnosis.

Manual Tracking — The Underrated Option

Manual tracking — paper scorecard plus post-round entry into a spreadsheet or Golfmetrics — produces as good or better data quality than GPS apps, costs nothing, and keeps your phone out of your routine during the round.

Getting Started — Your First 8 Rounds

Whatever tool you choose, the first 8 rounds are calibration data. Do not draw conclusions before round 8. Do not change your practice based on 2–3 rounds of tracking data — the sample is too small to be meaningful.

The 8-Round Rule

Minimum Sample for Meaningful SG Data

  • 1
    Rounds 1–3: Focus entirely on consistent data collection. Do not interpret the numbers — just gather them. Inconsistent recording undermines all future analysis.
  • 2
    Rounds 4–8: Begin reviewing category averages. Look for the category with the most negative SG (or the worst proxy stat). This is your primary improvement target. Cross-reference with the relevant guide.
  • 3
    Round 8+: First meaningful review. Update Guide 10 (Your Milestone Plan) with your actual data. Set a 3-month target for the weakest category. Review again at round 16.
The Single Most Important Principle

Consistency Over Sophistication

8 rounds of manual scorecard tracking entered consistently into a spreadsheet is worth more than 3 rounds of Arccos data with incomplete post-round correction. The tool matters less than the consistency of use. Choose the simplest tool you will actually use for every round, and upgrade when you are genuinely ready to act on more granular data.
